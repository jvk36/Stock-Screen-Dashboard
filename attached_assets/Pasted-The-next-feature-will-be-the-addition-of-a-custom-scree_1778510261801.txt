It needs parameters across 6 domains, each covering a different dimension of stock analysis – So, a check-box group that allow selecting multiple domains from the 6, with each one having a set of filters should suffice: 1. Valuation — "Am I paying a fair price?": Filters - a) P/E (trailing) — 0–100x (median S&P ~22x; deep value <10x, growth >40x), b) Forward P/E — 0–60x, c) PEG ratio — 0–5.0 (GARP zone: <1.5; expensive: >3), d) P/B (price-to-book) — 0–10x (asset-heavy value: <1; capital-light quality: 3–8x), e) EV/EBITDA — 0–30x (value: <8x; fair: 8–15x; rich: >20x), f) P/FCF (price-to-free cash flow) — 0–60x (value zone: <15x), g) Dividend yield — 0–10% (income focus: >3%; dividend growers: 1–3%), 2. Growth — "How fast is this business growing?": Filters – a) EPS CAGR (3yr) — 0–60% (floor for GARP: 15%; hypergrowth: >30%), b) Revenue CAGR (3yr) — 0–80%, c) EPS CAGR (5yr) — 0–50%, d) Forward EPS growth (next 12mo) — 0–50%, e) Revenue growth (next 12mo) — 0–50%, f) Dividend CAGR (5yr) — 0–30% (meaningful above 7%), 3. Profitability & quality — "Is the business fundamentally excellent?": Filters - a) ROE — 0–100%+ (good: >15%; exceptional: >30%; FICO-level: >100% due to buybacks), b) ROIC — 0–60% (wide moat threshold: >20%), c) Gross margin — 0–100% (SaaS/pharma: >70%; retail: 25–40%; manufacturing: 20–35%), d) Operating margin — 0–50%, e) Net margin — 0–40%, f) FCF margin — 0–50%, g) FCF conversion (FCF/net income) — 50–150% (>90% = high quality), 4. Financial health — "Can it survive and compound?": Filters – a) Debt/Equity — 0–5.0x, b) Debt/EBITDA — 0–6.0x (investment grade: <3x; distressed: >5x), c) Current ratio — 0.5–5.0 (healthy: >1.5), d) Interest coverage — 0–30x (safe: >5x; distressed: <2x), e) Payout ratio — 0–100% (sustainable dividends: <60%), 5. Momentum & technicals — "What is the market currently doing with this stock?": Filters – a) 12-1 price momentum — -50% to +150%, b) 4-week return — -20% to +40%, c) RSI (14-day) — 20–80 (oversold: <30; overbought: >70; GARP sweet spot: 40–65), d) % from 52-week high — -80% to 0% (deep value territory: <-40%; momentum: -5% to 0%), e) % above 200-day MA — -30% to +30%, f) Volume Z-score — 0–4σ (breakout signal: >1.5σ), g) Relative strength vs S&P (3mo) — -20% to +30%, 6. Special signals — "Are there confirming signals beyond the numbers?" (Some of these are binary toggles rather than ranges): a) Insider buying (last 6 months), b) Active buyback program, c) Earnings beat streak (min quarters: 1–8), d) Analyst estimate revisions (% upgraded, 0–50%), e) Short interest (>10% = potential squeeze; also a risk signal), f) IV rank <30 (for LEAPS/asymmetric plays), g) Dividend increase streak (1–60 years), h) Margin direction (expanding / stable / contracting), i) 52-week high territory (within 5%), j) Morningstar wide moat designation. Note: For the ranges, consider keeping them open-ended so as to enable capturing the extremes.
